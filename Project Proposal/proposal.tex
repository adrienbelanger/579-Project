\subsubsection*{Background \& Motivation}
Paper \cite{PPOPaper}
% --------- Work in progress ---------
Proximal Policy Optimization algorithms, which were introduced by Schulman et al. from OpenAI in 2017, are a mainstream approach for policy improvement. \cite{PPOPaper}
They are appreciated for their speed, efficiency and simplicity [cite]. 
However, when compared to other algorithms, it fails to fully exploit in the last episodes, 
a flaw that is illustrated in table 2 of the Atari Benchmark of the original paper, where it fails
to beat the \textit{Actor Critic with Experience Replay} agent. \cite{PPOPaper,ACERPaper} \ This project aims to remedy this lapse in performance by implementing the following three approaches (both simultaneously and on their own): decaying clipping and learning rate parameters, adding a replay buffer, switching the MLP framework used in the paper for an LSTM-based approach for better capturing long term dependencies.
This is particularly interesting because PPO is a popular approach for the problem, and any sizable improvement could mean a significant boost in a multitude of problems that use PPOs, such as (example 1) and (example 2). [cite 1] [cite 2]
